## How to work with me

👋 I'm a mix of **German and Italian** — structure and precision on one side, creativity and improvisation on the other. Sometimes that feels like mixing oil and water.

Expect directness, a structured mindset, curiosity, eagerness for innovation, and occasionally dry (usually bad) humor and memes.

**Integrity, reliability, consistency, curiosity, and innovation** guide how I approach work and collaboration.

Integrity means **personal trust and openness**. The best idea should win, regardless of who proposed it.

💬 **Direct communication works best.** If something looks wrong or could be improved, say it openly and ideally talk to me directly rather than using indirect loops or waiting for formal feedback cycles.  
If feedback ever feels too direct or my communication style doesn’t work well for you, please say so.

📝 **Async and written communication** is usually preferred. It keeps decisions clear and documented.

⚡ Moving forward quickly and iterating often works well, depending on the risk and impact of a decision.

🤝 Working with people who **keep commitments and collaborate consistently** makes teams and systems much stronger.

Work tends to move **very fast**, sometimes with a bit of impatience — slowing things down when needed is always welcome.

🔍 Small details and broken things tend to stand out quickly. This can be helpful, but sometimes means focusing on issues others may not have noticed yet.

💡 Technical disagreement is healthy — the goal is always to **discuss options and find the best solution together**.

Happy to help with **architecture, operational issues, recurring problems, cross-team topics, or complex situations** — even outside engineering. When helping others, guidance is usually preferred over immediately providing answers.

---

### How to get the best out of me

- **Be direct and transparent.** Honest discussions make collaboration easier and faster.
- **Bring ideas and challenge assumptions.** Strong opinions and thoughtful disagreement usually lead to better solutions.
- **Share context early.** Understanding the broader problem space helps make better technical decisions.
- **Move forward and iterate.** Progress and learning from feedback is often better than waiting for perfect answers.
- **Raise issues early.** Small problems are easier to solve before they grow into larger ones.

---

### Things that can be frustrating

Like everyone, there are a few patterns of collaboration that tend to slow things down or make work harder:

- **Unreliability or inconsistent follow-through** — keeping commitments is important for trust and momentum.
- **Indirect communication or avoiding difficult conversations** — discussing issues directly usually resolves them faster.
- **Politics over substance** — decisions work best when the strongest idea wins.
- **Slow feedback loops for small decisions** — quick discussion and iteration often works better than waiting for formal cycles.
- **Ignoring small problems** — small broken things tend to accumulate and eventually create bigger issues.
